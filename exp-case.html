Designing (content) for better User Engagement

June 2024

PROJECT OVERVIEW

How much content is there on the internet? A recent study by the BBC Science Focus magazine gave us an estimated number.

1.2 Million Terabytes

Ill give a moment for the number to sink in.

But among the other objectives of this article, the impact of those numbers arent something that we are comprehending here. Well, maybe not directly. Well see where our objective leads us to.

The real question this piece of text would be investigating while surviving in a swamp full of 138 crore people is: how much of the content is accessible to the general public. The term general public is another subjective deal, which transforms itself as we move up or down the social, economic and the caste ladder. A great deal of Marxist manifesto would break the silence for the ones who cares to listen.

Accessibility, in the context of UX design describes the usability of the creative piece, it being inclusive to all its users. Although for a digital business venture in an Indian context, the trade-off between accessibility and profit making is often high, if not always. But the bar has seen its rise in the past decade to a respectable and workable level.

At Testbook, we curate content, bridge them to our users and assess its retention among them. The establishment does the same via conducting Live classes, follow-up study sessions, practice and notes distribution, assessments through tests, quizzes and series of them to complete the cycle of learning. The main vision while focusing on the assessment aspect of it, the company accelerated its growth in the past couple of years to rise to the top of all platforms that provide learning facilities for government exam aspirants.

But here it hardly gets lonely at the top. With the rise of ed-tech platforms in the country the strive to be the best can get tricky. Complex problems arise with the changing trends in design and the market. Revisiting strategies and introspection are a part of the drill.

Building a narrative in the sea of content
